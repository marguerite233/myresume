Lab of Prof. LI XIAOCHUAN

Graduation Thesis - Fractal theory on porous media

Porous media, which are functional composite materials, have been widely used in scientific researches and industrial productions, for example, heat-mass transfer and insulation materials. And the complex and irregular structure of porous media, which is difficult to describe in Euclidean geometry, can be analyzed by fractal theory. The key in fractal analysis is to obtain the fractal dimension D of porous media. Among various methods, the box counting method is the most popular one to obtain the fractal dimension. However, there is no standard or objective way to carry out the box counting method, resulting in different estimation of D under different algorithms of the method.

In the study, three traditional algorithms of box counting method are discussed and a modified algorithm are proposed to enhance the accuracy and stability of D estimation.

Lab of Prof. QI YONGFENG

Research Apprentice - Low temperature SCR catalyst

The nitrogen oxides emitted by coal-fired power plants are harmful to both ecosystem and human body. Currently, the selective catalytic reduction (SCR) of NOx with ammonia has been proved to be the most efficient technology for removing NOx from power plants. Among various catalysts which have been reported to be active for the SCR reaction, the V2O5–WO3/TiO2 catalyst is the most mature one, having been commercialized for many years. In order to avoid the deactivation caused by SO2 and dust (fly ash), in coal-fired power plants, the SCR reactor is suggested to be located downstream of the precipitator and the desulfurizer where the temperature is below 200°C. However, commercial catalysts only show high activity above 320°C and low-temperature catalysts synthesized at laboratories are limited in practical use because of their instability and high cost. Based on the reality, a method is put forward that spent commercial catalysts are utilized to make new catalysts by adding some substances for widening the operating temperature range.

In the study, three samples of V2O5–WO3/TiO2 catalysts used for more than 18000 hours were collected from power plants in China, and flue gas parameters were offered by these power plants, aiming at revealing the influence of the industrial flue gas parameters on micro morphologies and elements valence distributions of SCR catalysts. Furthermore, one of used catalysts was taken as the support to make Mn-Ce-Cr catalyst and its performance was discussed based on catalytic activity test, SO2 resistance test and characterizations.

Now, the paper is still being submitted and examined.

Lab of Prof. QI YONGFENG

Research Apprentice - Decoking technology in boiler system

This project aimed at designing a device for quantitatively evaluating decoking efficiency. Dcoking technique is hot topic in thermal engineering for the reason that the alkali and alkaline-earth metals in coal and biomass lead to low melting point of fly ash by combustion in boiler systems, resulting in fly ash easily solidified on tubes as coke which can reduce heat transfer efficiency and cause burst of tubes.

In previous studies, seniors in lab tried to compare outcomes of different synthesized decoking agents by adding them to test tubes which containing cokes. Obviously, it was a primitive method because the outcomes were compared only by sense of sight without quantitative data.

Therefore, I finished designing a system for evaluating decoking efficiency under the guidance of Prof. Qi and Prof. Wang and the assistance of graduate student Ge. Then we applied for a patent.
