Undergraduate Thesis

Fractal theory on porous media

Porous media have been widely used in researches such as heat-mass transfer and insulation materials. And the complex and irregular structure of porous media, which is difficult to describe in Euclidean geometry, can be analyzed by fractal theory. Among various methods, the box counting method is the most popular one to obtain the fractal dimension (D) which is an key indicator in fractal theory. However, there is no standard or objective way to carry out the box counting method, resulting in different estimation of D under different algorithms.

In the study, three traditional algorithms of box counting method are discussed and a modified algorithm are proposed to enhance the accuracy and stability of D estimation. Moreover, an application was designed by MATLAB APP DESIGNER to realize easy Human-Computer Interaction.

Research Apprentice

Low temperature SCR catalyst

The three scrapped industrial V2O5–WO3/TiO2 catalysts, which had operated in three coal-fired power plants with various flue gas conditions in China, were collected and characterized by XRF, XRD, SEM, and XPS to analyze their deactivation mechanisms, with the complexities of various industrial flue gases from coal combustion considered. Then one scrapped industrial catalyst was selected as the carrier to prepare a new catalyst by adding Mn, Ce, and Cr species, for SCR reactions in flue gas with as well as without SO2 at much wider temperature range.

This paper was written by myself with instruction of Prof. Yongfeng Qi and published on waste and biomass valorization. (DOI:10.1007/s12649-020-01158-3)

Research Apprentice

Decoking technology in boiler system

Decoking technique is hot topic in thermal engineering for the reason that the alkali and alkaline-earth metals in coal and biomass lead to low melting point of fly ash by combustion in boiler systems, resulting in fly ash easily solidified on tubes as coke which can reduce heat transfer efficiency and cause burst of tubes.Therefore, various decoking agent were systhesized but how to evaluate those agents is still a issue.

In this project, I finished designing a system for quantitative evaluating decoking efficiency under the guidance of Prof. Qi and Prof. Wang and the assistance of graduate student Ge. Then we applied for a patent in 2017 (Publication No. CN107942009A).
